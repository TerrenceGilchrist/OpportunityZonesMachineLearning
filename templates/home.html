Social Science Examines Opportunity Zones

Historical Background

In 2015, a white paper by economists Jared Bernstein and Kevin A. Hassett was published by the Economic Innovation Group. The document reviewed federally funded placed-based programs and the authors suggested private capital as an alternative. The concept was introdued February 2, 2017 by U.S. Senators Tim Scott (R-SC) and Cory A. Booker (D-NJ) as legislation called the Investing in Opportunity Act. In December 2017, the legislation was enacted as part of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act. In April 2018, the IRS and U.S. Treasury Department designated 8,764 census tracts as Opportunity Zones. Yet, federal regulations for Opportunity Zones were not finalized until December 2019. During that time, a range of investments into those census tracts occured, some of which were consistent with the intendment of the Opportunity Zones legislation while others were not.
